Decision Making (Evaluation)

E** is for Evaluation or Decision-making. This is judgement, the ability to decide, and foresight. It is comparison and contrast, deciding likenesses and differences. E** is required in situations that involve reasoning one's way through incomplete information, whether ambiguity or uncertainty. It deals primarily with the practical side of life. Unfortunately, it is difficult to teach in a classroom, so schooling usually does not nurture or build E** abilities as much as would be desirable. The E** measure is a composite of five different MindCue activities.

E** abilities are normally not fully developed until one approaches adulthood. Accordingly, it should not be alarming if young people do not score high in this area. Further, it is not hard to acknowledge and understand the sometime irrational behavior of some teenagers when this fact is taken into account.

Stanine 6 and above are the people who take responsibility, plan efficiently, and make good decisions. High E** people tend to make judgements for other people. They typically want to be in control, are often leaders, and they like to organize and strategize. Supervisors, foremen, administrators, and executives generally score well in E** abilities. However, when the high E** become managers, their keen sensitivity to nuances (especially when C** is also high) may make them too sensitive and even vulnerable to manipulation by others.

Stanine 3 or below are often "in trouble" because they make wrong decisions and are poor planners. They often freeze when faced by ambiguous or uncertain information. Until middle-aged or so (when physical aging may seem to show "wisdom"), they may be considered immature because they do not know how to make decisions.

Evaluation of Figural Classes (EFC)

"Judging Similarities¾ Matching Attributes & Concepts"

 

 

 

 

Directions: Click the object that belongs with or has the most in common with the objects on the left.

 

 

 

Ability to: Decide if physical objects not directly related are correctly sorted.
Expected to: Be well-organized and efficient in use of spatial information to solve problems where answers do not have a direct one-to-one relationship.
Undeveloped: May dislike or have difficulty in jobs estimating or requiring responsibility for keeping materials organized; may not comprehend concepts easily; problems with similarities and differences; low reading comprehension.

EFC measures Evaluation of Figural Classes, or judgement of similarities among spatial objects. EFC judges how well one classifies visual information.

Stanine 7 and above indicates an ability to make good judgements about belongingness. This ability allows the person to be well-organized and efficient in both classroom and workplace environments.

 

Stanine 3 or below indicates that the person has trobule sorting items of visual information based on their shared properties. Because so many important tasks require classification abilities, the person will be limited in education and career prospects until the **C abilities are developed.

 

Decision Making(Evaluation)

Evaluation of Symbolic Relations (ESR)

"Judging Symbolic Relations"

 

If a<b and b<c then,

Directions: Click the correct statement based upon the information above.

Ability to: Decide the correctness of relationships between units of symbolic data.
Expected to: Be efficient in the correctness, logical order, and priority of symbolic data that have been grouped or paired. Correctly apply these relationships to problem-solving situations.
Undeveloped: May have difficulty in understanding the relationships between ordered symbolic data and may find use of equations and formula extremely challenging.

ESR measures Evaluation of Symbolic Relations, or the ability to see relationships between units of symbolic information. ESR judges a person's ability to deal with formula and equation relationships. It measures the ability to make logical use of established relationships to create and use new relationships.

Stanine 7 and above indicates the person is well versed in numerical data and operates within that realm with ease. Can easily select the appropriate ordering of symbolic operations to solve mathematical problems. Should excel in the higher levels of mathematics, engineering, and scientific pursuits.

Stanine 3 or below indicates difficulty in placing numerical data in a logical order in which to solve a problem. Usually has difficulty in understanding equations and formula, and will also have trouble assembling a string of symbolic notation that represents the problem faced. This person should avoid courses or jobs that require tasks that involve substantial amounts of algebra, geometry, or trigonometry.

Evaluation of Symbolic Systems (ESS)

"Judgement of Correctness of Symbolic Facts"

 

 

 

Directions: Click the set of numbers that conforms to the rule.

Ability to: Judge which procedure to use, in correct order, for solving problems generated by data or computer programs, or other tasks requiring sequencing.
Expected to: Efficient at solving problems that require judgement and planning in data-dependent tasks.
Undeveloped: Unable to make correct decisions where procedures are implied.

ESS measures Evaluation of Symbolic Systems, or judgement of correctness among symbolic data. ESS judges how well one systemizes symbolic material.

Stanine 7 and above indicates an ability to make appropriate decisions about which processes to use, and in which order, in basic problem-solving situations requiring symbolic data.

Stanine 3 or below indicates that the person has difficulty making decisions about which processes to use, or in which order to use them, in basic problem-solving situations requiring symbolic data. The person may be susceptible to "math anxiety" due to inability to handle ambiguity.

 

Decision Making(Evaluation)

Evaluation of Symbolic Implications (ESI)

"Symbolic Problem-Solving"

 

 

Directions: Which hotel should Bob use if he likes to swim and has Master Charge?

Ability to: Translate and employ symbolic information in problem-solving.
Expected to: Efficiently read symbols, translate their meaning and then employ that meaning in solving a problem.
Undeveloped: Unable to understand and use complicated symbolic information.

 

ESI measures Evaluation of Symbolic Implications, or judges how well an individual can gain information from a symbolic source, translate the information, and then apply that information in problem-solving. The ability to locate oneself on a map or to read services listed in a catalog are heavily dependent on ESI ability.

 

Stanine 7 and above indicate the person is very familiar with symbolic material and operates well in sources where most information is represented via symbol. Understands and uses the concept of acronyms, even maybe to a fault. Prefers brevity and most likely is a strong user of information and the Internet.


Stanine 3 or below indicates the individual has difficulty employing symbolic information in problem solving. If S** ability is low, but **I ability is high, then the problem is more oriented towards dealing with symbolic material in general, and not specifically in the problem-solving area. Persons with this under-development will most likely have trouble dealing with computers and information technology.

Decision Making(Evaluation)

Evaluation of Semantic Systems (EMS)

"Judging the Correctness of Nuance"

Directions: Which of these stories is true?

1. A boy fell off the bike and hurt his leg.

2. Someone is riding a bike and is waving.

Ability to: Judge the presence, correctness, and appropriateness of information implied in a semantic statement.
Expected to: Listen to or read semantic information and make correct deductions and assumptions regarding that information and make further use of them.
Undeveloped: Unable to see and understand nuance. View situations as clear black or white choices, does not see an area in between the choices.

EMS measures Evaluation of Semantic Systems, or judges a person's ability to see and understand information that is conveyed or suggested by semantic information. EMS measures the ability to understand a point of view or to "read between the lines".

 

Stanine 7 and above indicates the individual is very perceptive and understands what is being said and what is not being said. This person usually enjoys reading and can mentally transport themselves into the environment they are reading about. If other **I scores are also high, the person is usually considered by others to be very intuitive, if not even clairvoyant.

 

Stanine 3 or below indicates an inability to fully understand what is being said or read. Although non-verbal information may be present, this person does not fully understand its meaning and relationship to the semantic information being processed. Usually, this person is very choice oriented and does not understand the concept of a "gray area". If **I is low, the individual may have trouble dealing with intuition in general and cannot effectively deal with cause and effect type thinking. If CMS is low, the cause of low EMS may be related to poor processing of semantic information.
