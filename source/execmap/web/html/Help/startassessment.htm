Begin Assessment

The assessment consists of 13 timed activities. It starts with two pages of instructions and legend to screen control buttons. There is a voice over covering the instructions also. Located in the instructions is a check off box to determine if the user’s system has sound. This is defaulted to sound capable.

Each activity is timed with the time remaining shown in the upper right hand corner. At the expiration of this time, the activity may automatically closed and answers thus far, submitted to the database. If you finish the activity before the time expires, you should be given the opportunity to go back and review questions/answers or close the activity, submit answers, and go on to the next activity.

At the completion of each activity, a brief motivation message as well as statistics concerning how many more exercises and task time is required to complete the assessment is shown.

At the completion of the entire assessment, congratulatory message may appear that includes the fact that reports will be delivered once his/her company has completed the assessment period.

You can take the assessment in one session or many sessions. At any point, you can stop the assessment and return later to complete the remaining activities.When you re-enters the system, it should take you to the activity that follows the last activity for which valid scores were recorded.

Each activity starts with an instruction page outlining how the activity is to be accomplished. In most cases, there is an example question with the correct answer noted and a brief description as to why that is a correct answer. You may quit at this stage or proceed to the activity. Onceyou start the activity, the timing commences. On each screen during the activity a control button allowing the individual to return to the instruction page is present, although the timing continues on while the individual is reviewing the instructions.

Although activities vary from one to the other, each questions is stated and the possible answers are displayed. Each answer choice should be set such that it shows an at rest condition, a hover condition, as well as a selected condition. Proceeding from one question to the next is controlled by the NEXT button. There is also a BACK button should the individual wish to review the previous question. Also located on the screen are question markers or symbols showing a number for each question. These markers should indicate which questions have been answered and the current question through color changes. They can also be used as navigation back to the specific question should the user want to review or change an answer.

Following activities shall be displayed in assessment:-

 

EMS: In this activity, you will see a picture. Look at the picture carefully and read the given options. Then select the options that best express what is happening in the picture.
CFS:In this activity, you will see a picture of blocks. The blocks can be viewed from different directions: Front, Side, and Top. Your Task is to click the view that is correct for the direction indicated.
CMI: In this activity, you will be presented with a set of statements and a conclusion. You are given the option to state whether the conclusion is true, not true, or unable to conclude. For each question, select the appropriate choice.
CSR: In this activity, each pair of words or numbers are related by the letters or numbers inside them. You have to specify the word that comes in place of the question mark.
NST: In this activity, the names of various fruits are hidden in the sentences. Try to find them. Click at the start and the end of the name of the fruit, when you find it. Be careful, there may be more than one in some sentences.
NMT:In this activity, y ou will be shown pair of sentences. You must decide whether the two sentences within a pair have similar or dissimilar meanings. Remember that not all pair of sentences are equally similar or dissimilar. Some might be very similar or dissimilar. Click the correct choice.
ESS:In this activity, read the rule in the box, then find the series that conforms to that rule. Look carefully there may be more than one correct series. You must specify all the correct answers
EFC: In this activity, you will see a group of objects that are related in some way. Click the answer choice that belongs to or is related to the group of objects in the same way.
MEM: In this activity, you will see or hear some numbers or statments. Study them carefully. After a brief delay you will be asked to perform some task using the information you studied. Once the next question or answer selection of the corresponding question appears , you are not allowed to view the previous question/answer.
CMS:In this activity, using the given symbols, click the image that means the same as the given paragraph.
ESC:In this activity, A team of climbers are trying to get as HIGH up the mountain as they can. On the mountain, there are six camps-each with a rule. Each Climber has a number and they can climb to the highest camp where their number conforns to the rule. Remember, try to get each climber as high as possible.
DMS:In this activity, click the statement you would like to add to this sentence. As soon as an answer is given, the second set of statements for that question should appear with the instruction: Click the statement you wish to add to end this thought. Once the next question appears , you are not allowed to view the previous question.
NFU: In this activity, on the right side of the screen you will see a red ball and an up and down arrow. Once you start the exercise, the ball will begin to move. Using the up and down arrows,keep the ball in the center of the scale.At the same time,the numbers and letters in the bx at the lower left will constantly change.Periodically, the exercise will stop and you will be asked to recall the numbers or letters that are currently in the box
