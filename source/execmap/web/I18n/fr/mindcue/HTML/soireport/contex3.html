The Five Thinking Operations

The Structure of Intellect Model (SOI) defines five thinking operations, each of which describes the operation of the mind on basic information.

Cognition Cognition is the most basic of thinking processes. It is known as "comprehension" and "understanding" and includes discovery, awareness, rediscovery, or recognition of information in various forms. High = Quick; one time learner.

MemoryMemory is retention and retrieval of information in any form. It is one of the easiest to train of all the thinking abilities. High = Recalls everything well.

Decision-Making Decision Making is critical thinking. It is deciding or judging correctness, suitability, adequacy, desirability, etc., of information. It assesses consistency and goal satisfaction. It is a thinking ability that is frequently overlooked in schools. High = Practical, manages time well.

Problem Solving Problem solving is also critical thinking. It synthesizes new information from given information, emphasizing what is conventionally accepted as the best answer or outcome. School work and business usually favor problem solving. High = Task oriented.

Creativity Creativity brings new information from given information, emphasizing the variety and originality of answers. Originality, flexibility, and fluency are important components of this ability. High = Innovative, fluent, humourous, high energy.

The Three Contents of Thinking

The Structure of Intellect Model (SOI) also defines three formats of thinking, each of which is a general variety of information.

Figural Figural information is the real thing. It is what we can see, hear, and touch directly. It may be a photo, a sound, or a gesture. It may be visual, auditory, or kinesthetic. This is a very important component for beginning learners. High = Good spatially, manipulates and figures mentally or manually.

Symbolic Symbolic information is in the form of symbols or signs. These are arbitrary marks that have no significance in or of themselves, but to which society has attached meaning. Examples include numeric and alphabetic characters, musical notes, and the various triangles, semicircles, etc., used on weather maps. The phonic approach to reading is symbolic content. High = Manipulates numbers or letters well; good with music.

Semantic Semantic information is found in the meaning that society attaches to words. It is either reading or writing, listening, or speaking. It tends to deal with the abstract. High = Good with words and ideas; express themselves well.

The Six Products of Thinking

The Structure of Intellect (SOI) model defines six products of thinking, each of which is obtained through the intellect’s processing of information.

Units A unit is a single item, such as one figure, one symbol, or one word. High = Good with details.

Classes Classes are sets of information units, grouped by common properties. High = Well organized.

Relations Relations are the connections that we recognize between units of information, based upon variables that link them. High = See connections.

Systems Systems are organized patterns of information items. They are arrangements of parts that interrelate or interact. Examples of systems include sequences of mathematical operations and the structure of language. High = Sees the big picture.

Transformations Transformations are changes to known information or its use. They require that the information be redefined or modified. High = Able to see varying points of view.

Implications Implications deal with cause and effect. They start with known information, then seek back to unknown causes or forward to unknown effects. High = Anticipates well.

Reading the Trigrams:

SOI Paradigm of Intelligence

Each Trigram is made up of one Operation, followed by one content, followed by one product. Thus CFU stands for Cognition of Figural Units. EMT stands for Evaluation of Semantic Transformations.

In this manual, the * means "all." So N** means Convergent Production in all contents and all products. *M* means All operations in Semantic content for all products.

Understanding Stanines:

Stanine scores are a set of nine section where the distribution of a normal curve is ranked for educational testing. These sections are sized so that the top section (Stanine 9) and the bottom section (Stanine 1) are 4% and the middle section (Stanine 5) is 20% and all sections have a standard deviation of 2 and a mean of 5.
