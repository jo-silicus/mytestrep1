Divergent Production (Creativity)

D** is for Divergent Production. Sometimes called creativity, this is the ability to generate, construct, or elaborate solutions within the boundaries of a problem. Factors of one’s D** score are fluency (producing quickly and in volume), variety (producing in great diversity), originality (producing what is beyond the conventional or expected), and transformation (producing new uses for the known). In contrast to N** , the D** ability diverges from the known, out to the unknown. The D** measure is accomplished by one activity.

Stanine 6 and above is most rare. High D** people are the creative ones in society. They show humor, are flexible, original, and often quality conscious. Typically, they have such high energy levels – when working on tasks they like – that they become single-minded. High D** very flexible, so they become impatient with those who are inflexible or intolerant of alternative work styles. They do not like routine work. They are usually independent workers who see their own ways to work with data and solve problems. They often will not work for supervisors who they think are unfair; in school, they often will not work on subjects that they do not like.

Stanine 3 and below is not a disadvantage in the everyday world because the majority of tasks do not depend on D** abilities. Low D** are usually not fluent producers of many new or different ideas. They often have high anxiety levels when faced with novel situations.

To achieve high scores in all areas of D** is extremely unexpected; few people do so, although some do measure very high in at least one area. Often a high score in the D** activity completed indicates a preference for that content area and forms an indication of the area where the individual would succeed the most.

DFU measures Creativity or Divergent Production of Figural Units, or simply graphic creativity. DFU judges the ability to originate new graphic designs. This is the ability required by tasks where creativity with shapes and thins is critical – the arts, carpentry, design, costuming, landscaping, cartooning, remodeling, architecture, etc.

Stanine 7 and above indicates comfort with ambiguous tasks. The person enjoys drawing sketches, designs, or graphs to express new ideas. The person prefers to give and receive information in *F* format, whether for school, business, or pleasure.

Stanine 3 or below indicates a person who may appreciate *F* information, but prefers it sequenced, symmetrical, patterned, determinate - in contrast to the high DFU person, who often prefers the asymmetrical and the indeterminate. The person probably avoids situations where original *F* information must be produced.
