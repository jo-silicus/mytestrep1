Memory

 

M** is for Memory. This is the ability to commit information to storage for exact retrieval. Memorization may be either a conscious or unconscious effort. And memory itself is either short-term or long-term. Short-term memories come and go, as when a person looks up a phone number and remembers it only long enough to dial it. In this example, long-term memory is where that short-term memory is transferred after the brain recognizes that same phone number is being frequently retrieved. The M** measure is a composite of many different MindCue activities.

Stanine 6 and above show a rapid ability to recall facts. They often come to the attention of instructors because they seem so knowledgeable. They tend to commit everything to memory easily and without practice.

Stanine 3 and below need much practice before they store information. They may be auditory or visual learners. High MS*auditorywith low MS*visual will identify the auditory person: about 15 percent of people tend to

work more quickly when material is presented orally. These people will work best with oral directions or explanations. High MFU and MMI with low MS*auditory people will work more quickly when information is explained and presented visually as well. High MS*visual and low MS*auditory identifies the visual person: about 80 percent of people tend to be visual. They require written instructions and explanations. Low M** people over over-represented among the "educationally handicapped."

Memory for Figural Systems (MFS)

"Visual Recall of Interrelationships of Figural Systems"

Directions: Remember this map.

 

Ability to: Remember the interrelationships between parts of figural system.
Expected to: Easily remember how parts of a figural system fit together.
Undeveloped: Difficulty with geography and working with charts, layouts, blueprints, etc.

MFS measures Memory for Figural Systems, or memory of figural information that is interrelated. MFS judges how well one can not only recall the individual parts to a system, but also how they fit together and function as a system. MFS is a critical ability for architecture, construction, map making, etc.

 

Stanine 7 and above indicates the individual has an exception ability to remember the interrelationships between parts of figural information. Geography, mechanical drawing, engineering as well as weather forecasting and graphic designs are all areas where this person would excel given the other abilities required for the task are in place.

Stanine 3 or below indicates the individual would have difficulty in remembering how individual pieces of figural information fit together. Working with sequential tasks where each task completed must be remembered and then reintroduced as a part of another, more complicated task, would be challenging for this person. If **S ability is low, the problem could be related more to understanding the concept of systems as opposed to one of memory.

Memory for Symbolic Units (MSU)

"Symbolic Detail Recall/Attending¾ Visual & Auditory"

                                                                   

Directions: Remember these numbers but leave out the 3.

Ability to: Remember abstract visual and auditory information.
Expected to: Easily recall sequences of visual and auditory information
Undeveloped: Difficulty with spelling may lose concentration easily.

MSU measures Memory for Symbolic Units, or memory for symbolic details. Visual MSU judges how well one attends to symbolic information presented visually. Auditory MSU judges how well one attends to symbolic information presented orally.

Stanine 7 and above indicates an ability to attend to symbolic information. The person is well able to pay attention to, and concentrate on, detailed information presented in coded form. If visual MSU and MSS are high, but auditory MSU and MSS are low , the person is a visual learner. If the situation is the other way around, the person is an auditory learner.

Stanine 3 or below indicates a disability in attending to symbolic information. The person has trouble paying attention to, or concentrating on, detailed information presented in coded form. Because nearly all tasks require some memory for details, the person's attention span should be developed to a higher level. If auditory MSU and MSS abilities are both low, the person may need an auditory exam. Low visual MSU and MSS abilities suggest the need for a developmental vision exam.

Note: The visual portions of these activities are vision indicators.

Memory for Symbolic Systems (MSS)

"Symbolic Sequences Recall/Sequencing¾ Visual & Auditory"

Directions: Remember these numbers and put them in backwards order, but leave out the 8.

Ability to: Remember and process accurately sequences of visual and auditory information. Necessary for instruction and for following work routines.
Expected to: Correctly keep this information in mind, without interruption, while processing more information.
Undeveloped: Will not be able to "hold" and process information presented visually or auditorily, or respond accurately to additional directions given at the same time.

MSS measures Memory for Symbolic Systems, or memory for symbolic sequences. Visual MSS judges how well one processes symbolic information presented visually. Auditory MSS judges how well one processes symbolic information presented orally.

Stanine 7 and above indicates an ability to process symbolic information. The person is well able to process detailed information presented in coded form. If visual MSU and MSS are high, but auditory MSU and MSS are low the person is a visual learner. If the situation is the other way around, the person is an auditory learner.

Stanine 3 or below indicates a disability in holding symbolic information in mindf while working with it. The person has trouble processing detailed information presented in coded form. Because nearly all tasks require the ability to keep details in mind as they are being used, the person's attention span should be developed to a higher level. If auditory MSU and MSS abilities are both low, the person may need an auditory exam. Low visual MSU and MSS abilities suggest the need for a developmental vision exam.

Note: The visual portions of these activities are vision indicators.

Memory for Semantic Implications (MMI)
"Memory for Verbal Implications"

 

Directions: Which one would each person see?

 

Ability to: Remember and recall strings of abstract implied information. Necessary for problem-solving based on deductions from partial information.
Expected to: Recall unrelated, abstract information and see implications. Will take initiative.
Undeveloped: Will not enjoy working with highly abstract, unrelated information. Will make poor inferences. May not work well independently.

MMI measures Memory for Semantic Implications, or memory for inferential meaning. MMI judges how well one stores cause-effect meaning in memory.

 

Stanine 7 and above is rare. A high MMI ability indicates that the person will work well in drawing inferences, or making logical deductions, from incomplete information. If NSI is also high, the person may seem intuitive and will do well in making plans and decisions based on partial information.

 

Stanine 3 or below indicates that the person will not enjoy working with highly abstract information where relationships are not spelled out. The person will not be able to "hold" unconnected facts in mind until the implication of those facts is found. The person will make poor inferences from incomplete information, and may not succeed at tasks that require independent decision-making.
