Problem-Solving (Convergent Production)

 

N** stands for coNvergent production. For the Mindcue product, we call it problem-solving. It is the ability to arrive at a determinate answer from given information. This is one's ability to use information or facts to achieve a defined or desired outcome. The N** measure is composed of three different Mindcue activities.

Stanine 6 and above indicates academic potential and is usually associated with high grades in school and college, and with research and scholarship. High N** also reflects general working skills and production abilities. The person who can solve problems where answers are determined by rule-following will like tasks that are predictable, where work is well documented, and where completion objectives are known ahead of time. High N** with high D** will also work hard, but prefer tasks that interest them.

Stanine 3 and below usually do not have good work skills, nor will they stick with subject matter that they deem as tedious. Low N** may stem from low M** . They are not skillful in situations where they must adjust existing rules to new circumstances. Requiring varied motivation programs, they can be encouraged by behavior modification systems.

Convergent Production (Problem Solving)

Convergent Production of Symbolic Systems (NSS)

"Application of Symbolic Rules"

In a recent year, China launched 5 satellites, and the USA launched 3 times as many. Israel launched only a quarter as many as France, which launched three fewer than the USA. How many satellites did Israel launch.

Directions: In this activity, you will figure the answer to a word problem involving arithmetic. Read each word problem carefully, then select the correct answer from among the answers shown in the boxes below the statement.

Ability to: Determine correct solutions to problems requiring numbers, codes, or formulae.
Expected to: Quickly, accurately, apply symbolic facts or rules to solve problems.
Undeveloped: Unable to keep systems of data in mind for reproducing expected solutions.

NSS measures convergent production or problem solving of Symbolic Systems. This means the ability to apply symbolic rules in problem solving. NSS judges how quickly and accurately one applies "math facts" to solve problems.

Stanine 7 and above indicates an ability to work with complicated tasks that require intense concentration, follow-through, and pulling together many kinds of information. A high NSS ability depends on CSS and ESS ; e.g., a calculator can do CSS, but the person needs high NSS to master the calculator.

Stanine 3 or below, coupled with low CSS , indicates lack of skills in simple arithmetic. The person will experience difficulty "seeing" arithmetic solutions. However, if auditory MSS is also low, then the problem may be poor hearing or low attention abilities.

 

Problem Solving (Convergent Production)

Convergent Production of Symbolic Transformations (NST)

"Speed of Word Recognition"

Directions: Click between the letters to identify the hidden fruit name

Ability to: Correctly revise symbolic information to produce new information. Necessary for reading, including computer reports, and data entry.
Expected to: Rapidly read or scan data, items, or words.
Undeveloped: Will lose place while reading; skipping words or lines.

NST measures convergent production or problem solving of Symbolic Transformations, or the speed of data recognition. NST judges how quickly and accurately one can scan or read items and words. (The speed factor in reading is separate from one’s *M* abilities.)

Stanine 7 and above indicates an ability to read and scan data quickly. If

C** and *M* are also high, then the person should be very efficient at tasks where information must be processed and understood quickly and accurately. If CMU is low, then vocabulary building is in order.

Stanine 3 or below, coupled with low EFU , indicates poor visual discrimination. This suggest the need for a developmental visual exam and correction before attempting tasks that demand constant visual discrimination.

 

 

Problem Solving(Convergent Production)

Convergent Production of Semantic Transformations (NMT)

"Interpreting Nuance"

The teacher praised the student.
The student was praised by the teacher.

Similar     Dissimilar

Directions: In this activity, you will see two sentences. You task is to determine if the two sentences mean the same thing. If they mean the same thing, click "similar", if they do not mean the same thing, then click "dissimilar".

Ability to: Correctly revise semantic information to produce new information
Expected to: Recognize minor differences or nuance in a semantic system.
Undeveloped: Will have difficulty understanding and appreciating stories and literature.

 

NMT measures convergent production or problem solving of Symbolic Transformation, or the ability to perceive and understand nuance. NMT judges ones ability to receive semantic information and interpret the true meaning of the information. A person with high NMT is usually a very perceptive individual.

Stanine 7 and above indicates an ability to read "between the lines" or interprets "body language" when judging the meaning of semantic information. They often see the true meaning behind the words and understand the meaning and impact of "what is not being said, but is meant."

Stanine 3 or below indicates difficulty in understanding exactly what is being conveyed. Normally, they are very literal persons who see the world from a very rigid perspective. They do not account for "gray" areas or understand emotion.
