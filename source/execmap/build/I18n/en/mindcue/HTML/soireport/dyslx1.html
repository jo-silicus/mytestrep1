During this student's assessment, many different scores indicated problems with vision and reading. As the two are closely related in this assessment, it is difficult to determine the actual cause of the problem. This raises the possibility of dyslexia. This assessment is not a valid instrument for determining the existence and type of dyslexia and it should not be used as a sole tool for determining whether a student suffers from yslexia or not. Still, the preponderance of low visual and reading scores indicates that further screening for this dysfunction would be advisable.
